ABOUT ME


Hi, I'm Shuchi!

I am a Ph.D. student in statistics at the University of California, Los Angeles and and trainee at the Bureau of Intelligence and Research at the U.S. Department of State. I am broadly interested in statistical methodology and applications in demography and social network analysis. My dissertation work, advised by Dr. Mark S. Handcock, focuses on two-sided matching processes in social networks. For the past two summers I have also worked as a research intern for Facebook's infrastructure organization, and I am an affiliate of the California Center for Population Research..

Through computational social sciences research, I aim to study relationship dynamics and family outcomes within hidden populations. This includes studying the migration patterns of minority groups or the spread of addiction in isolated communities. Ultimately, I hope to use this knowledge to help make technology and educational resources more accessible and useful to diverse populations.

I graduated from the University of Georgia in 2018 with a B.S. in statistics and a B.A. in economics, along with a history minor and a data science certificate. At UGA, I had the privilege of being supported by the Foundation Fellowship.

Other things that make me happy: all types of puzzles (word, logic, jigsaw), women's advocacy, Georgia football, singing, and fixed-width fonts.

Research

Revealed preferences modeling

  • Propose model which derives latent personal preferences for partners separably from availability
  • Apply model to two-sided matching problems (i.e. marriage, medical residency programs, employment)
  • Run simulation studies showing consistent accuracy and precision of preference parameter estimates

Missing data analysis in public health surveys

  • Consult for APA Health CARE initiative investigating public health outcomes for minority Americans
  • Conduct exploratory data analysis to determine collinearity between various SES characteristics
  • Determine and apply appropriate multiple imputation methodology for missing data
  • Mitigate effects of collinearity among variables when fitting logistic regression model

[To the left] Correlation matrix of demographic characeristics (made with ggplot2)

Robust small area estimation for mixed populations

  • Proposed re-parametrized model for small area estimation, using hierarchical Bayes approach
  • Applied model to estimate agricultural production in Iowa counties given limited sample sizes
  • Demonstrated that proposed model produced narrower confidence intervals
